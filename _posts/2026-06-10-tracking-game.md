This is a small browser experiment that mirrors the kind of task I use in my research on brain–machine interfaces: you have to do two things at once.

- **Track** the moving target with your cursor (or your finger, on a phone).
- **Match** the target's color at the same time — click (or tap) to switch colors.

It's a toy, but it gets at something real: how well can a person split attention between a continuous control task and a discrete decision? That trade-off is exactly what co-adaptive interfaces have to reason about. The cursor is my husky, because of course it is.

<!--more-->

Try it and see how long you can keep both going at once.
